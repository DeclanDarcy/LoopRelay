## Certification Posture

Milestone 0.4 is certifiable as a Phase 0 decision governance foundation.

The certified architectural claim is narrow:

- architecture-affecting change types are defined with decision rules, required evidence, compatibility impact, regression impact, rollback, and durable documentation obligations,
- architectural evidence packages are required for decisions, mechanisms, certification, acceptance, rollback, and baseline updates,
- compatibility and rollback are governed before broad migration starts,
- active M0.4 governance artifacts, evidence links, capability claims, and mechanism claims have executable reachability guards,
- regression weakening, shell mirror inventory drift, authority/projection-like file names, and compatibility structures have initial governance detectors.

The review does not claim complete historical decision corpus validation, automatic semantic quality review of decisions, full source-change detection for every architecture-affecting edit, compatibility derivation correctness, passive transport certification, generated contract certification, semantic authority restoration, or downstream acceptance.

## Justified Claims

- M0.4 defines the architectural decision process for authority, projections, contracts, compatibility, regressions, generated artifacts, transport, state, controller/workspace boundaries, runtime failure scope, and reference architecture changes.
- Evidence is required as the basis for decisions, mechanisms, certification, acceptance, rollback, and baseline updates.
- Rollback and compatibility are governed before broad contract, transport, authority, and frontend migrations begin.
- Initial executable guards protect the M0.4 governance artifacts and selected high-risk governance bypasses from silent drift.
- Certification claims remain bounded to governance foundation readiness.

## Unjustified Claims

- Full validation of every historical decision or evidence file.
- Automated review of whether a human-authorized decision is substantively correct.
- Complete source-change detection for every possible architecture-affecting edit.
- Compatibility field derivation correctness, consumer migration completeness, or retirement readiness.
- Passive transport, generated contract ecosystem, semantic authority restoration, state ownership, controller/workspace architecture, or runtime isolation certification.
- Downstream acceptance or baseline publication of the full architecture.

## Known Limits

No blocker requires another M0.4 governance-concept expansion slice before certification.

Accepted limitations are intentionally scoped:

- historical decision files remain traceable but not fully schema-validated,
- decision quality remains a governance responsibility, not an automated verdict,
- compatibility correctness remains later contract, transport, authority, and structural-debt work,
- M0.4 acceptance and baseline closeout remain separate from certification.
