# Reasoning Authority Boundary

Reasoning Trajectory is explanatory. It can support, influence, challenge, and explain decisions and project direction, but it is not an authority surface.

## What Reasoning May Do

- Explain why thinking changed.
- Preserve why an alternative, assumption, contradiction, or direction mattered.
- Link events across decisions, milestones, handoffs, governance findings, operational-context revisions, and execution outputs.
- Reconstruct narratives from auditable evidence.
- Expose provenance for claims about reasoning history.
- Surface unresolved or recurring explanatory threads for human review.

## What Reasoning May Not Do

- Approve or reject decisions.
- Resolve, supersede, or archive decisions.
- Promote operational context.
- Replace `.agents/operational_context.md`.
- Enforce governance findings.
- Create execution directives.
- Start, reuse, or resurrect provider sessions.
- Become a private knowledge database.
- Store raw conversation transcripts as authority.
- Treat graph edges or reconstructions as current project state.

## Interaction With Decisions

Decisions remain authoritative for decisions. Reasoning may explain why a decision replaced earlier thinking or why an alternative was rejected, but decision state belongs to Decision Lifecycle services and artifacts.

## Interaction With Operational Context

Operational Context remains authoritative for current settled understanding. Reasoning may preserve how that understanding emerged, but it does not decide what is settled and does not mutate operational context.

## Interaction With Governance

Governance remains responsible for detecting current issues and contradictions. Reasoning may preserve the history of those findings and how they influenced later work.

## Interaction With Execution

Execution remains responsible for execution context, provider process lifecycle, handoff validation, commit, and push workflow. Reasoning may explain execution influence, but execution directives and repository workflow state remain outside Reasoning Trajectory.

## Certification Boundary

Reasoning certification should prove that important reasoning questions remain answerable from repository artifacts. It must not certify that a decision is correct, that a governance finding is enforced, or that operational context is complete.
